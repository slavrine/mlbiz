WEAR AND TEAR

Use survival analysis to predict when a pipeline segment would develop a leak ahead of the time. Utilize inventory data on valves, pipe segments and weak points to understand different pipe segments' strength and corrosion resistance. Use the history of pressures, volumes, fluid types and leaks to train the model.

In addition to pipe-specific data, soils and hydrogeology in the pipe vicinity are considered. Closest road crossings, waterways/lakes and weather (pipe freezing/defrost) are taken into account.

Other examples of such technology would include wear and tear prediction on drillstring cables in hydraulic fracturing operations, where not only mileage is considered, but well fluids, speed of travel, well shape and many more.

Such technology is applicable to any engineered part wear and tear prediction. Based on relevant engineering specifications, the nature of the environment the part is in and the historical record of usage+failure, we can train a model capable of such prediction.

The predictions do not specify a concrete time of failure of the part since installation. Rather, they are given in terms of probability of failure over time. This has additional benefits, as we can use it to analyze historical part usage. For example, if a part has a sharp rise in failure probability in it's mid-life, this could mean that this part suddenly encounters a rough, wear-prone environment. This can be analyzed further and the environment can be modified to prolong the part life.
